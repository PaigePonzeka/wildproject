WHAT THE CONSTITUTION MEANS TO ME
written by Heidi Schreck
directed by Oliver Butler
June 21 – July 1 @ 8pm
(No show June 27th)
June 29 @ 4pm

It’s 1989 and Heidi is trying to earn enough money to go to college by giving speeches about the Constitution in American Legion Halls across the country. When she loses the first round to Becky Dobbins because her speech isn’t personal enough, she decides to go deep. Starting with her great great grandmother, a mail-order bride who died of “melancholia,” she traces the effects of a single sentence of the Ninth Amendment on generations of women in her family – and on the violent men they married.

Rachel Hauck (set design), Michael Krass (costume design), Jennifer Schriever (light design), Sinan Zafar (sound design), Heather Robb (composer), David Neumann (choreographer), Ashley-Rose Galligan (production stage manager)

With: Heidi Schreck, Danny Wolohan and Rosdely Ciprian

Clubbed Thumb commissions, develops and produces funny, strange and provocative new plays by living American writers. Clubbed Thumb is a groundbreaker, with a precise curatorial vision and a remarkable track record for launching artists’ careers; and an incubator, nurturing plays, collaborations, and above all artists, through thoughtfully deployed resources, opportunities, mentorship and hospitality.

Clubbed Thumb’s plays vary in style and content, but are always 90 minutes or under. They feature substantial and challenging roles for both men and women, are questioning, formally inventive, theatrical, and exhibit a sense of humor. Since its founding in 1996, the company has presented nearly 100 productions, and has been awarded 5 Obies, including the Ross Wetzsteon award for sustained excellence.
