TASTE
Direction by Jay Palmieri Jr.
37 mins (NYC)
Thriller. Winner of 18 Awards.

When Roe (Christina Toth), a bulimic fashion model, moved to New York she thought her dark family secrets were finally behind her, but after a chance encounter with Evan (Danielle Kronenberg), a young fashion photographer with a history of sexually manipulating her models, Roe finds that her animalistic ways can't be tamed after all.

When a young man suffering from an eating disorder is pressured to stay for breakfast by his boyfriend, the couple are thrust into an otherworld where their anxieties and frustrations are made horrifyingly real. RIBBONS
direction by Brandon Cordeiro
9 mins (NYC)
trailer: vimeo.com/179660266
In the directorial debut of local filmmaker, Brandon Cordeiro In 1997 a mother brings her eight-year-old son to a public AIDS memorial on a beach in Provincetown, Ma. Provincetown, a small harbor village at the very tip of Cape Cod, Massachusetts, was already a home to the LGBT community for a few decades when AIDS devastated its population in the mid- 1980s. ribbons is a short film that explores an episode in the life of a second generation Portuguese boy when his mother brings him to a community memorial service by the sea. The boy experiences firsthand the loss incurred by the epidemic, but also his community’s inspirational response to it; an event that as an adult helps him understand the power of forming a bond with a kindred tribe.
BETTAS
direction by Benjamin Shalom-Rodriguez
8 mins (NYC)
On vacation for a friend's wedding, boyfriends Francisco and David get into a trivial argument that quickly escalates. BETTAS stars Jose Diaz-Oldenburg and Nick Ley.

After an eye-opening night out with a friend, a sister must decide whether to save her sister, or save herself. Everyone needs a Savior. Two aging showgirls, one giant moth, one helluva night in Bridgeport.
PRIA
directed by Yudho Aditya
21 mins (US/Indonesia)
trailer: priashortfilm.com
In a time of great expectation and pressure stemming from the traditions of his upbringing, Aris-a gay Muslim teenager living in rural Indonesia-finds himself increasingly drawn to his well-traveled English teacher despite his upcoming marital preparations to a woman he harbors no romantic feelings for. With his whole life planned out for him by his overprotective mother, Aris struggles with the conflicting notions of tradition, duty and his own personal idea of happiness.
