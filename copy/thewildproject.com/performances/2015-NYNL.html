New York No Limits Film Series Short Film Event 3

showtimes:

August 20th @ 7:30pm

New York No Limits continues the year round short film series with eclectic film fair from at home and across the pond. The 6 films to be presented on August 20th exhibit that range and depth of filmmakers who use skill and storytelling on a meager budget is the perfect antidote to summer blockbusters.

Short Film Lineup

DON'T FORGET TO TIP
Gal Nachshon director experimental/U.S. The independent art industry seems to be working for tips, from street musicians to poets, and filmmakers alike.

KARKASS KARTS
Ben Nelson director horror/comedy/U.S. When the Birch brothers inherit a failing funeral home from their decaying father, they must resort to sinister means in order to save the family business.

NYC I-X
Kenneth Anderson director, experimental documentary/U. S. NYC I-X is an experimental documentary that utilizes the ten sign semiotic system of Charles Sanders Peirce by adopting New York City as object, New Yorkers as interpretant, and ten different scenes as the signs themselves.

PARDON THE INTRUSION
Louise Galizia director narrative/U.K. Betty, unable to speak due to a stroke, finds herself stuck in a frightening situation when a mysterious figure begins living in her home. After sleepless nights, she is finally confronted. A teenage runaway, Grace, has become brazen enough to move in permanently, avoiding the carers detection. Grace discovers the fight in this elderly woman may just be the role model she has been searching for, and Grace, the company Betty so desperately desires.

VIOLETS
Jim Vendiola director horror/U. S. Two strange, reclusive sisters quietly await the advent of their secretly twisted plan. Inspired by true events.

YOU'RE BREAKING UP
Mandy Fabian director comedy/U.S. An eager bride overhears a disturbing conversation on her wedding day that forces her to make a life-changing decision.

The program is approximately 80 minutes in length. A brief Q & A will occur at the conclusion of the program with the filmmakers in attendance.

NYNL launched in November 2010 as a platform for filmmakers who create films that come directly from the filmmakers vision devoid of commercial influence.
