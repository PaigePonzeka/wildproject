WORKING THEATER DIRECTORS SALON

showtimes:

March 17-18 & 20-22 @ 7pm

tickets:

Free

Join us for a week of events created to inspire and assist emerging stage directors in their careers. Throughout the salon, directors will be given the opportunity to work, network, hear from leaders in the field and join a community of both emerging and established directors. The salon is now in its fifth season, and it will be the biggest salon to date!

Tuesday, March 17, 2015 at 7:00pm
DISCUSSION: Directors' Roundtable
hosted by the Salon Curators

A relaxed, peer-led discussion with fellow directors on everything from challenges to opportunities.

Wednesday, March 18, 2015 at 7:00pm
Q&A PANEL: "Not Waiting for the Ask"
MEET THE PANELISTS

Join in a discussion with Rachel Chavkin, Alec Duffy, and Daniel Jaquez about creating your own opportunities.

Friday, March 20, 2015 at 7:00pm
PERFORMANCE: Alumni Night
with Special Guest Artists Anna/Kate
MEET THE ALUMNI

Featuring work by Lillian Meredith, Emerie Snyder, Shana Solomon, Illana Stein, Daniela Thome, and Dina Vovsi.

Saturday, March 21, 2015 at 7.00pm
PERFORMANCE: Director-Driven work
MEET THE DIRECTORS

One of the most exciting parts of the Salon is our night of director-driven work. We put out a call for submissions and we choose three projects to receive rehearsal space, resources, support, and a public presentation. Join us Saturday to see a brand new project in front of an audience for the first time! The evening ends with a talkback with all the artists, so you can be a part of the process. This year's directors are John Michael DiResta, Jerry Ruiz, and Emma Weinstein.

Sunday, March 22, 2015 at 7.00pm
PERFORMANCE: 7 new plays based on the prompt "to serve & protect"
MEET THE PLAYWRIGHTS

The culmination of the Salon week is the night of brand new plays by some of the hottest playwrights in New York, and all the directors are chosen out of a hat at the Kick-Off Party! This year's directors are Anouk Kemp, Nilan Johnson, Claire Beckman, Alex Mallory, Christopher James Weihert, Jeremy Karafin, and Nathaniel P. Claridad.
